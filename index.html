★ Laylatul Qadr Dua — Most Important Dua of Ramadan ★
Taught by the Prophet ﷺ to Aisha رضي الله عنها — specifically for Laylatul Qadr
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي

Sayyid al-Istighfar — Master Dua of Forgiveness
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allāhumma Anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā Anta, khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka, wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿtu, aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿtu, abūʾu laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya, wa abūʾu laka bidhanbī faghfir lī, fa innahu lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā Ant
"O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I am upon Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me and I acknowledge my sin. So forgive me, for no one forgives sins except You."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306) — Say morning & evening: dies that day → enters Paradise
Dua of Yunus (AS) — Al-Anbiya 21:87
لَّا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
Lā ilāha illā Anta subḥānaka innī kuntu minaẓ-ẓālimīn
"There is no god but You. Glory be to You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."

No Muslim supplicates sincerely with this except Allah answers — al-Tirmidhi (Sahih)

Morning AdhkarAfter Fajr · Seal the morning with remembrance
The morning adhkar are a comprehensive shield for the day. Begin after Fajr salah, ideally before sunrise. They take 10–15 minutes and provide protection, forgiveness, and gratitude all in one.
  • 1
    Ayatul Kursi — 1×
    اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ...
    Recite Ayatul Kursi in full (Al-Baqarah 2:255). A guardian is appointed; Shaytan cannot approach until evening.
    Source: al-Nasāʾi, ʿAmal al-Yawm wal-Laylah (960)
  • 2
    Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas — 3× each
    قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۞ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۞ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ
    Recite each surah 3 times. Protection from all harm for the morning.
    Source: Abu Dawud (5082), al-Tirmidhi (3575)
  • 3
    Sayyid al-Istighfar — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ...
    The Master of Forgiveness. Whoever says this in the morning believing in it, and dies that day, enters Paradise.
    Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306)
  • 4
    Protection from All Harm — 3×
    بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
    Bismillāhil-ladhī lā yaḍurru maʿa ismihi shayʾun fil-arḍi wa lā fis-samāʾ wa Huwa as-Samīʿul-ʿAlīm
    "In the name of Allah with whose name nothing on earth or in heaven can cause harm. He is the All-Hearing, All-Knowing."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5088), al-Tirmidhi (3388) — Nothing will harm him that morning
  • 5
    Contentment & Gratitude — 3×
    رَضِيتُ بِاللَّهِ رَبًّا وَبِالْإِسْلَامِ دِينًا وَبِمُحَمَّدٍ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ نَبِيًّا
    Raḍītu billāhi Rabbā wa bil-Islāmi dīnā wa bi-Muḥammadin ṣallallāhu ʿalayhi wa sallama nabiyyā
    "I am pleased with Allah as my Lord, with Islam as my religion, and with Muhammad ﷺ as my Prophet."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5072) — It is incumbent upon Allah to please him
  • 6
    Morning Declaration — 1×
    أَصْبَحْنَا وَأَصْبَحَ الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ...
    Aṣbaḥnā wa aṣbaḥal-mulku lillāh, wal-ḥamdu lillāh, lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah...
    "We have entered the morning and the dominion belongs to Allah... My Lord, I ask You for the good of this day and I seek refuge in You from the evil of this day, from laziness, the misery of old age, the punishment of the Fire, and the punishment of the grave."
    Source: Sahih Muslim (2723)
  • 7
    Protection from the Fire — 7×
    اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي مِنَ النَّارِ
    Allāhumma ajirni minan-nār
    "O Allah, protect me from the Fire." — The Fire will not touch him that day.
    Source: Abu Dawud (5079)
  • 8
    For Complete Wellbeing — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ الْعَفْوَ وَالْعَافِيَةَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ ۙ اللَّهُمَّ اسْتُرْ عَوْرَاتِي وَآمِنْ رَوْعَاتِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ احْفَظْنِي مِنْ بَيْنِ يَدَيَّ وَمِنْ خَلْفِي وَعَنْ يَمِينِي وَعَنْ شِمَالِي وَمِنْ فَوْقِي
    Allāhumma innī asʾalukal-ʿafwa wal-ʿāfiyata fid-dunyā wal-ākhirah · Allāhummastur ʿawrātī wa āmin rawʿātī · Allāhummaḥfaẓnī min bayni yadayya wa min khalfī wa ʿan yamīnī wa ʿan shimālī wa min fawqī
    "O Allah, I ask You for pardon and wellbeing in this world and the next. O Allah, conceal my faults and calm my fears. O Allah, protect me from in front of me, behind me, from my right, my left, and from above me."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5074), Ibn Majah (3871)
  • 9
    Health in Body, Hearing & Sight — 3×
    اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي بَدَنِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي سَمْعِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي بَصَرِي ۙ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
    Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī badanī · Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī samʿī · Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī baṣarī · lā ilāha illā Ant
    "O Allah, grant me health in my body. O Allah, grant me health in my hearing. O Allah, grant me health in my sight. There is no god but You."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5090)
  • 10
    Gratitude for Blessings — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ مَا أَصْبَحَ بِي مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ أَوْ بِأَحَدٍ مِنْ خَلْقِكَ فَمِنْكَ وَحْدَكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ ۙ فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ وَلَكَ الشُّكْرُ
    Allāhumma mā aṣbaḥa bī min niʿmatin aw bi-aḥadin min khalqik, fa minka waḥdaka lā sharīka lak, fa lakal-ḥamdu wa lakash-shukr
    "O Allah, whatever blessing I or any of Your creation have reached this morning, is from You alone — no partner. To You belongs all praise and all thanks."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5073) — Whoever says this has given thanks for the day
  • 11
    Tahlīl — 100×
    لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ ۙ لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
    Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah, lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamd, wa Huwa ʿalā kulli shayʾin Qadīr
    "There is no god except Allah alone, no partner. To Him belongs the dominion and all praise, and He has power over all things." — 100 good deeds, 100 sins erased, protection from Shaytan all day.
    Source: al-Bukhari (3293), Muslim (2691)
  • 12
    SubḥānAllāh wa biḥamdih — 100×
    سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ
    Subḥānallāhi wa biḥamdih
    "Glory be to Allah and His praise." — Sins forgiven even if like the foam of the sea.
    Source: al-Bukhari (6405), Muslim (2692)
  • 13
    Allāhumma bika aṣbaḥnā — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا وَبِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا وَبِكَ نَحْيَا وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ النُّشُورُ
    Allāhumma bika aṣbaḥnā wa bika amsaynā wa bika naḥyā wa bika namūtu wa ilaykan-nushūr
    "O Allah, by Your grace we have reached the morning, and by Your grace we reach the evening. By Your grace we live and die, and unto You is the resurrection."
    Source: al-Tirmidhi (3391), Abu Dawud (5068)
  • 14
    Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm — 1×
    يَا حَيُّ يَا قَيُّومُ بِرَحْمَتِكَ أَسْتَغِيثُ أَصْلِحْ لِي شَأْنِي كُلَّهُ وَلَا تَكِلْنِي إِلَىٰ نَفْسِي طَرْفَةَ عَيْنٍ
    Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm, bi-raḥmatika astaghīth · Aṣliḥ lī shaʾnī kullah, wa lā takilnī ilā nafsī ṭarfata ʿayn
    "O Ever-Living, O Sustainer of All, by Your mercy I seek help. Rectify all my affairs, and do not entrust me to myself even for the blink of an eye."
    Source: al-Nasāʾi in ʿAmal al-Yawm wal-Laylah (46)

Before SleepSunnah sequence · 3 minutes · every night
Step 1 — Ayatul Kursi (Al-Baqarah 2:255)
اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَّهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
Allāhu lā ilāha illā Huwal-Ḥayyul-Qayyūm · lā taʾkhudhuhu sinatun wa lā nawm · lahu mā fis-samāwāti wa mā fil-arḍ · man dhal-ladhī yashfaʿu ʿindahu illā bi-idhnih · yaʿlamu mā bayna aydīhim wa mā khalfahum · wa lā yuḥīṭūna bishayʾin min ʿilmihī illā bimā shāʾ · wasiʿa kursiyyuhus-samāwāti wal-arḍ · wa lā yaʾūduhu ḥifẓuhumā · wa Huwal-ʿAliyyul-ʿAẓīm
"Allah — there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Throne extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (2311) — Allah appoints a guardian; Shaytan cannot approach you until morning

Qunoot al-Witr — Authenticated Supplication of Witr
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّا نَسْتَعِينُكَ وَنَسْتَغْفِرُكَ وَنُؤْمِنُ بِكَ وَنَتَوَكَّلُ عَلَيْكَ ۙ وَنُثْنِي عَلَيْكَ الْخَيْرَ ۙ وَنَشْكُرُكَ وَلَا نَكْفُرُكَ ۙ وَنَخْلَعُ وَنَتْرُكُ مَنْ يَفْجُرُكَ ۞ اللَّهُمَّ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ ۙ وَلَكَ نُصَلِّي وَنَسْجُدُ ۙ وَإِلَيْكَ نَسْعَى وَنَحْفِدُ ۙ وَنَرْجُو رَحْمَتَكَ ۙ وَنَخْشَى عَذَابَكَ ۙ إِنَّ عَذَابَكَ بِالْكُفَّارِ مُلْحِقٌ
Allāhumma innā nastaʿīnuka wa nastaghfiruka wa nuʾminu bika wa natawakkalu ʿalayk · wa nuthnī ʿalaykal-khayr · wa nashkuruka wa lā nakfuruk · wa nakhlaʿu wa natruku man yafjuruk. Allāhumma iyyāka naʿbudu · wa laka nuṣallī wa nasjud · wa ilayka nasʿā wa naḥfid · wa narjū raḥmatak · wa nakhshā ʿadhābak · inna ʿadhābaka bil-kuffāri mulḥiq
"O Allah, we seek Your help and ask Your forgiveness, and we believe in You and rely on You. We praise You with all goodness, and we thank You and we are not ungrateful to You. We disown and abandon those who disobey You. O Allah, You alone we worship, and to You we pray and prostrate. To You we hasten and we serve. We hope for Your mercy and we fear Your punishment. Verily, Your punishment shall overtake the disbelievers."
Source: Abu Dawud (1425), al-Bayhaqi — Sahih — Narrated from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (RA) as the dua the Prophet ﷺ taught for Witr
When to recite: In the final rak'ah of Witr prayer, either before rukuʿ (raising hands with takbir after recitation) or after rukuʿ (before going into sujud) — both positions are established in the Sunnah. Raise your hands while reciting. Witr is one, three, five, or seven rak'ahs — the odd number that concludes your night prayer.

Tarawih & WitrNight prayer in Ramadan — structure, recitation & duas
What is Tarawih? Tarawih (تراويح) is the voluntary night prayer performed in Ramadan after ʿIsha. The Prophet ﷺ established it and the Companions later prayed it in congregation. It is a Sunnah Muʾakkadah — a confirmed Sunnah — not an obligation, but highly recommended. Do not leave it.
Standard Structure
Pray in sets of 2 rak'ahs, concluding with a single Witr. The established narrations support 8 rak'ahs (4 sets of 2 + Witr) or 20 rak'ahs (10 sets of 2 + Witr). Both are authentic. Follow your imam.
Each Set of 2 Rak'ahs
Make your intention for Tarawih. Recite Al-Fatihah + a surah in each rak'ah. Complete with tashahhud and taslim. Rest briefly between sets (this pause is the tarawih — "rest"). Then begin the next set.
Witr — The Seal
Pray 1 rak'ah (or 3, in which case pray 2 then 1 separately). In the final rak'ah, recite Dua al-Qunoot before or after rukuʿ. End with taslim. This closes your Tarawih and night prayer for the night.

Dua Between Tarawih Sets

After every 2 rak'ahs (i.e. during the tarawih rest period), recite the following glorification. It is narrated from the practice of the Companions and widely read in congregational Tarawih.

What to Recite in Witr

The Prophet ﷺ would recite specific surahs in Witr. If praying 3 rak'ahs: recite Al-Aʿlā (87) in the first, Al-Kāfirūn (109) in the second, and Al-Ikhlāṣ (112) in the third. He also sometimes added Al-Falaq (113) and An-Nās (114) in the third. — Abu Dawud (1423), al-Tirmidhi (462) — Sahih.

Ruqyah — Healing & ProtectionQur'an is a healing and a mercy for the believers
Ruqyah is the practice of reciting Qur'an and authentic supplications for healing and protection. It is an established Sunnah — the Prophet ﷺ performed ruqyah on himself and others. Recite over yourself by placing your hand on the area of ailment or over your chest. Recite into your cupped hands and blow gently, then wipe over the body. "We send down of the Qur'an that which is a healing and a mercy for the believers." (17:82)

Jumu'ah — Friday SunnahsThe weekly ʿEid of the believers
"The best day on which the sun has risen is Friday. On it Adam was created, on it he was admitted to Paradise, and on it he was expelled from it. And the Hour will not be established except on a Friday." — Sahih Muslim (854). Make it a day of special worship, abundant salawat, and Surah Al-Kahf.
When is this hour?
Opinion 1 — After ʿAsr until Maghrib — The stronger opinion held by ʿAbdullah ibn Salam (RA), Abu Hurayrah (RA), Imam Ahmad, and others. "Seek it in the last hour after ʿAsr." — Abu Dawud (1048), Sahih.

Opinion 2 — Between the two khutbahs — When the Imam sits briefly between the two parts of the Friday sermon. — Sahih Muslim (853).

Both are well-grounded. The safest practice is to make dua at both times.
Source: Bukhari (935), Muslim (852)

Qunut al-Nazilah — Dua for the Ummah in Times of Calamity
When the Muslim Ummah is suffering — from war, oppression, or widespread harm — it is a Sunnah for the Imam to recite Qunut al-Nazilah in Fajr (or other fard prayers) after ruku' in the final rak'ah, raising his hands in dua while the congregation says Ameen aloud. The wording is flexible; what follows is an established text narrated from the Prophet ﷺ.
