★ Laylatul Qadr Dua ★
Taught by the Prophet ﷺ to Aisha رضي الله عنها — specifically for Laylatul Qadr
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Allāhumma innaka ʿafuwwun tuḥibbul-ʿafwa faʿfu ʿannī
"O Allah, You are the Pardoner, You love to pardon, so pardon me."
Source: al-Tirmidhi (3513), Ibn Majah (3850) — Sahih
Say in every sajdah · on your commute · before sleep · after every salah
Sayyid al-Istighfar — Master Dua of Forgiveness
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allāhumma Anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā Anta, khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka, wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿtu, aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿtu, abūʾu laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya, wa abūʾu laka bidhanbī faghfir lī, fa innahu lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā Ant
"O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I am upon Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me and I acknowledge my sin. So forgive me, for no one forgives sins except You."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306) — Say morning & evening: dies that day → enters Paradise

Verily, Your punishment shall overtake the disbelievers."
Source: Abu Dawud (1425), al-Bayhaqi — Sahih — Narrated from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (RA) as the dua the Prophet ﷺ taught for Witr

Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamdu wa Huwa ʿalā kulli shayʾin Qadīr
"There is no god but Allah alone, with no partner. His is the dominion and His is all praise and He is over all things capable."
Source: Tirmidhi (3585) — Hasan
The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best du'a is the du'a of the Day of Arafah and the best thing I and the Prophets before me have said is this." Repeat this abundantly throughout the entire day of Arafah.
Du'a of Yunus ﷺ
لَّا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
Lā ilāha illā Anta subḥānaka innī kuntu minaẓ-ẓālimīn
"There is no god but You. Glory be to You. Indeed I have been of the wrongdoers."
Source: Surah Al-Anbiya (21:87)
Say this on Arafah day and in any time of difficulty. Guaranteed to be answered — Allah rescued Yunus ﷺ from the belly of the whale with this du'a.
For Complete Forgiveness — Arafah
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Allāhumma innaka ʿafuwwun tuḥibbul-ʿafwa faʿfu ʿannī
"O Allah You are the Pardoner, You love to pardon, so pardon me."
Source: Tirmidhi (3513) — Sahih
Say this throughout Arafah day — especially in the afternoon. The du'a of Laylatul Qadr, equally powerful on the greatest day of the year.
Sayyid al-Istighfar — Master of Forgiveness
اللَّهُمَّ أَنتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنتَ
Allāhumma Anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā Anta khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿtu aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿtu abūʾu laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya wa abūʾu bidhanbī faghfirlī fa innahu lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā Ant
"O Allah You are my Lord, there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I uphold Your covenant as best I can. I seek refuge from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour and confess my sin — forgive me, for none forgives sins except You."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306)
The Master of Istighfar. Whoever says this in the morning with conviction and dies that day enters Jannah. Same for the evening. Say morning and evening of Arafah — and throughout the day.
For Good in Both Worlds — 1×
رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ
Rabbanā ātinā fid-dunyā ḥasanatan wa fil-ākhirati ḥasanatan wa qinā ʿadhāban-nār
"Our Lord give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter and protect us from the punishment of the Fire."
Source: Surah Al-Baqarah (2:201)
This was the most frequent du'a of the Prophet ﷺ on Arafah. It covers everything — dunya, akhirah, and protection from the Fire in three short lines.
For Comprehensive Forgiveness — 1×
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذَنْبِي كُلَّهُ دِقَّهُ وَجِلَّهُ وَأَوَّلَهُ وَآخِرَهُ وَعَلَانِيَتَهُ وَسِرَّهُ
Allāhumma ighfir lī dhanbī kullahu diqqahu wa jillahu wa awwalahu wa ākhirahu wa ʿalāniyatahu wa sirrahu
"O Allah forgive all my sins — the small and great, first and last, public and secret."
Source: Sahih Muslim (483)
Pour your heart into this on Arafah. Every category of sin covered — nothing left unasked. One of the most comprehensive forgiveness duas in the Sunnah.
For Protection from the Fire — 7×
اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي مِنَ النَّارِ
Allāhumma ajirnī minan-nār
"O Allah protect me from the Fire."
Source: Abu Dawud (5079) — Sahih
Say 7 times in the morning and evening of Arafah. Whoever says this 7 times — the Fire will say "O Allah protect this person from me." Morning and evening of every day.
For All Good — Comprehensive
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مِنَ الْخَيْرِ كُلِّهِ عَاجِلِهِ وَآجِلِهِ مَا عَلِمْتُ مِنْهُ وَمَا لَمْ أَعْلَمْ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الشَّرِّ كُلِّهِ عَاجِلِهِ وَآجِلِهِ مَا عَلِمْتُ مِنْهُ وَمَا لَمْ أَعْلَمْ
Allāhumma innī asʾaluka minal-khayri kullihī ʿājilihī wa ājilihī mā ʿalimtu minhu wa mā lam aʿlam wa aʿūdhu bika minash-sharri kullihī ʿājilihī wa ājilihī mā ʿalimtu minhu wa mā lam aʿlam
"O Allah I ask You for all good — immediate and future, known and unknown to me. And I seek refuge from all evil — immediate and future, known and unknown to me."
Source: Sahih Muslim (2721)
Covers every good you could ever need and every evil you could ever fear — in one du'a. Say this throughout Arafah day.
For Jannah and Protection from Fire
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ الْجَنَّةَ وَمَا قَرَّبَ إِلَيْهَا مِنْ قَوْلٍ أَوْ عَمَلٍ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ النَّارِ وَمَا قَرَّبَ إِلَيْهَا مِنْ قَوْلٍ أَوْ عَمَلٍ
Allāhumma innī asʾalukal-jannata wa mā qarraba ilayhā min qawlin aw ʿamal wa aʿūdhu bika minan-nāri wa mā qarraba ilayhā min qawlin aw ʿamal
"O Allah I ask You for Paradise and whatever brings me near to it, and I seek refuge from the Fire and whatever brings me near to it."
Source: Ibn Majah (3846) — Sahih
Say this explicitly on Arafah — with full heart. This is the ultimate ask. Ask for Jannah directly.
Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm — Arafah
يَا حَيُّ يَا قَيُّومُ بِرَحْمَتِكَ أَسْتَغِيثُ أَصْلِحْ لِي شَأْنِي كُلَّهُ وَلَا تَكِلْنِي إِلَى نَفْسِي طَرْفَةَ عَيْنٍ
Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm bi-raḥmatika astaghīth aṣliḥ lī shaʾnī kullahu wa lā takilnī ilā nafsī ṭarfata ʿayn
"O Ever-Living O Sustainer, by Your mercy I seek help. Rectify all my affairs and do not entrust me to myself even for a blink."
Source: Al-Nasa'i — Sahih
Especially powerful in the afternoon of Arafah when du'a is most accepted. Ask Allah to fix every single affair.
For Acceptance — Closing Du'a
رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّا إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Rabbanā taqabbal minnā innaka Antas-Samīʿul-ʿAlīm
"Our Lord accept from us — You are the All-Hearing the All-Knowing."
Source: Surah Al-Baqarah (2:127)
End every du'a session with this throughout Dhul Hijjah. The du'a of Ibrahim ﷺ after building the Ka'bah — seal every session asking for acceptance.

Laylatul Qadr
CARD 1 OF 5
THE NIGHT
What is Laylatul Qadr?
One night in the last 10 of Ramadan is worth more than 83 years of worship. Allah revealed the Qur'an on this night. The angels and Ruh descend.

"Peace it is until the emergence of dawn." — Surah Al-Qadr

Seek it on every odd night: 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, 29th.
THE DU'A
The One Du'a — Teach It to Everyone You Love
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Allāhumma innaka ʿafuwwun tuḥibbul-ʿafwa faʿfu ʿannī
"O Allah, You are the Pardoner, You love to pardon, so pardon me."

Say it in every sajdah, on your commute, before sleep, after every salah.
al-Tirmidhi 3513, Ibn Majah 3850 — Sahih
THE WORD
ʿAfw vs Maghfirah — Why This Word Matters
Maghfirah (مغفرة) = sins forgiven and covered.

ʿAfw (عفو) goes further — sins are completely erased, pardoned as if they never existed.

The Prophet ﷺ specifically taught this word for the most blessed night. Ask for ʿAfw constantly.
THE PRACTICE
How to Maximise the Night
Wake before Fajr for tahajjud. Make wudu. Pray 2 rak'ahs. Recite Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas ×3 each.

Make the Laylatul Qadr du'a in every sajdah. Pour out personal duas — your marriage, family, health, rizq, akhirah.

Give sadaqah. Read Qur'an.
THE PROMISE
Every Du'a Reaches Allah
"There is no Muslim who makes du'a containing no sin... except that Allah gives him one of three things: either his du'a is answered immediately, or it is stored for him in the Hereafter, or an equivalent evil is averted from him."

Not one du'a on Laylatul Qadr is ever wasted.
Ahmad — Sahih
⭐
Complete
Say the du'a of ʿAfw in every sajdah on the odd nights. Not one word reaches Allah and goes unanswered.

THE DAY
Friday — The Master of Days
"The best day on which the sun rises is Friday. On it Adam was created. On it he was admitted to Paradise. On it he was expelled from it. And the Hour will not occur except on a Friday."

Every Friday carries immense spiritual weight.
Sahih Muslim 854
THE RECITATION
Surah Al-Kahf — Read Before Maghrib
"Whoever reads Surah Al-Kahf on Friday, a light will shine for him between the two Fridays."

Read from after Fajr until Maghrib. The first and last 10 ayahs protect from the Dajjal.
Al-Hakim, Sahih by Al-Albani

Increase Salawat — It Reaches the Prophet ﷺ
"Send abundant salawat upon me on Fridays — your salawat are presented to me."

Say it abundantly from Fajr to Maghrib on Friday.

Sahih al-Bukhari 935

The Jumu'ah Sunnah Acts

Dhul Hijjah — Ten Days
CARD 1 OF 7
THE DAYS
Why These Days Are the Greatest
Allah ﷻ swears by them in Surah Al-Fajr: "By the dawn. And by the ten nights." — 89:1-2

Ibn Hajar said these days combine all major acts of worship — prayer, fasting, charity, and Hajj — which does not occur in any other days.

Ibn Abbas: "More beloved to Allah for good deeds than any other days."
THE ACTIONS
What to Do — The First 9 Days
Fast all 9 days if able — especially Day 8 (Tarwiyah) and Day 9 (Arafah).

Increase: Takbeer (Allahu Akbar), Tahmid (Alhamdulillah), Tahleel (La ilaha illallah), Tasbih (SubhanAllah).

Give sadaqah. Read Qur'an. Make tawbah. If you intend Qurbani — do not cut hair or nails from 1st Dhul Hijjah until after slaughter.
Sahih Muslim 1977
THE TAKBEER
Takbeer al-Tashriq — Wājib
اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ
Hanafi ruling: Wājib for every Muslim — male and female, travelling or resident — to say once after every fard salah.

Begins: Fajr of 9th Dhul Hijjah. Ends: Asr of 13th Dhul Hijjah. Total: 23 prayers.
THE DAY
Yawm Arafah — The Greatest Day
"The best du'a is the du'a of the Day of Arafah."

Fast this day — it expiates sins of the previous year and the coming year.

For non-pilgrims: spend the day in worship, du'a, fasting, dhikr, Qur'an.
Tirmidhi 3585 · Sahih Muslim 1162
EID AL-ADHA
Eid al-Adha — 10th Dhul Hijjah
Sunnah acts: ghusl, wear best clothes, eat nothing before Eid salah (opposite of Eid al-Fitr). Say Eid takbeer from Fajr of Arafah.

Greeting: Taqabbalallahu minna wa minkum — "May Allah accept from us and from you."

Do not fast on Eid — it is forbidden.
QURBANI
Qurbani — Udhiyah
Wājib for every adult Muslim of means (nisab holder) — Hanafi position.

Performed on 10th, 11th, or 12th Dhul Hijjah. One sheep/goat per household. One seventh of a cow or camel.

Distribute: one third for yourself, one third for family/friends, one third for the poor. Do not give the butcher his wage from the meat.
AYYAM AL-TASHRIQ
11th, 12th & 13th — Days of Remembrance
Days of eating, drinking, and remembering Allah. Fasting on these days is forbidden.

Continue Takbeer al-Tashriq after every fard salah until Asr of 13th.

"The days of Tashriq are days of eating, drinking, and remembrance of Allah."
Sahih Muslim 1141
🕋
Complete
These ten days come once a year. Fast, give, remember Allah abundantly, and make du'a on Arafah as if it may be your last.

THE DAY
What is Ashura?
The 10th of Muharram. When the Prophet ﷺ arrived in Madinah and found the Jews fasting, he asked why. They said: "This is the day Allah saved Musa from Pharaoh."

He ﷺ said: "We have more right to Musa than you" — and fasted it, and commanded the Muslims to fast.

THE FAST
The Fast and Its Reward
"Fasting on the day of Ashura — I hope Allah will expiate the sins of the previous year."

This expiation is for minor sins. Fast the 10th of Muharram. Adding the 9th is strongly recommended — the Prophet ﷺ intended to fast it the following year but passed away before it came.
Sahih Muslim 1162, 1134
THE SUNNAH
Fast the 9th Too — Why It Matters
The Prophet ﷺ said: "If I am alive next year, I will certainly fast the 9th."

Scholars say: fast the 9th and 10th together to differ from the Jewish practice. The 11th is also acceptable if you missed the 9th.
Sahih Muslim 1134
THE WARNING
Warnings — What Has No Basis
Mourning and lamentation on Ashura has no authentic basis from the Prophet ﷺ or the Sahabah. Specific foods or communal gatherings on Ashura have no basis.

The correct act: fast the 9th and 10th quietly, as an act of gratitude to Allah ﷻ for saving Musa (AS). Keep it simple and sincere.
📅
Complete
Fast the 9th and 10th of Muharram. Simple, sincere, and worth an entire year of expiation.

THE MONTH
The Neglected Month
Sha'ban falls between Rajab and Ramadan, so people neglect it. The Prophet ﷺ fasted more in Sha'ban than any month other than Ramadan.

"It is a month in which deeds are raised to the Lord of the Worlds, and I love for my deeds to be raised while I am fasting."

Use Sha'ban to prepare your heart for Ramadan.
Sahih al-Bukhari 1969 · Ahmad, Nasai — Sahih
THE NIGHT
The 15th of Sha'ban — Laylat al-Bara'ah
There are narrations about the 15th being a night of special mercy. While some scholars questioned their strength, the majority of Hanafi scholars — including Ibn Abidin and al-Tahtawi — stated that individual worship on this night is recommended based on the general principle of seeking good in blessed occasions.
THE PRACTICE
What to Do on the 15th — Hanafi Position
Spend the night in individual worship: nafl salah, Qur'an recitation, abundant istighfar, and personal du'a. Visit graves if able — the Prophet ﷺ visited al-Baqi on this night.

No congregational gatherings, no specific number of rak'ahs, no communal celebration has any authentic basis. Keep it simple and sincere.
THE PREPARATION
Preparing for Ramadan
Sha'ban is your runway for Ramadan. Establish a Qur'an reading habit now. Begin fasting Mondays and Thursdays. Increase salawat on the Prophet ﷺ.

Resolve any outstanding debts or family disputes before Ramadan begins. Let Sha'ban be the month your heart turns toward Allah before the greatest month arrives.
🌟
Complete
Don't let Sha'ban pass in neglect. Your deeds rise to Allah this month — let them rise while you are fasting and in worship.

EID AL-FITR
Eid al-Fitr — Sunnah Acts
Ghusl before salah. Eat something sweet before going — the Prophet ﷺ ate dates. Wear your best clothes. Go one route to the masjid, return another. Say Eid takbeer aloud on the way. Listen to the khutbah. Give sadaqah to family and friends.
EID AL-ADHA
Eid al-Adha — Sunnah Acts
Ghusl before salah. Do NOT eat before Eid salah — eat from your Qurbani after. Wear best clothes. Say Eid takbeer. Perform Qurbani on 10th, 11th, or 12th. Distribute meat correctly. Continue Takbeer al-Tashriq after every fard salah.
THE TAKBEER
The Eid Takbeer
اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ
Say it from Fajr of Eid day. Say it aloud while walking to the prayer. Men say it aloud; women quietly.
THE GREETING
The Correct Greeting
تَقَبَّلَ اللَّهُ مِنَّا وَمِنكُمْ
Taqabbalallāhu minnā wa minkum
"May Allah accept from us and from you."

This was the greeting of the Sahabah on Eid — Ibn Hajar confirmed its chain in Fath al-Bari. It can be combined with "Eid Mubarak" but this is the authentic greeting.
🎉
Complete
Eid is a day of gratitude, worship, and community. Celebrate with the Sunnah and say: Taqabbalallahu minna wa minkum.

WHAT IS RUQYAH
What is Ruqyah?
Reciting Qur'an and authentic supplications for healing and protection from illness, evil eye, jinn, and sihr. An established Sunnah — the Prophet ﷺ performed ruqyah on himself and others.

Recite over yourself by placing your hand on the area of ailment. Recite into cupped hands, blow gently, wipe over the body.
Sahih Muslim 2186
THE SEQUENCE
The Ruqyah Sequence — Step by Step
1. Surah Al-Fatihah ×7
2. Ayatul Kursi ×1
3. Surah Al-Ikhlas ×3
4. Surah Al-Falaq ×3
5. Surah An-Nas ×3

Blow into cupped hands after each, wipe over body head to toe. Can be performed morning and evening as protection.
THE DU'A
The Ruqyah Du'a of the Prophet ﷺ
اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَأْسَ اشْفِ أَنْتَ الشَّافِي لَا شِفَاءَ إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
Allāhumma Rabban-nāsi adh-hibil-baʾs, Ishfi Antash-Shāfī, Lā shifāʾa illā shifāʾuk, Shifāʾan lā yughādiru saqamā
"O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the affliction. Heal — for You are the Healer. There is no healing except Your healing, a healing that leaves no illness behind."

Place your hand on the area of pain, say 3× Bismillah, then ×7.
Bukhari 5675, Muslim 2191
DAILY PROTECTION
Morning & Evening Protection
The three Quls (Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas) ×3 after Fajr and Maghrib protect for the entire day/night.

Ayatul Kursi after every fard salah — whoever recites it, nothing will prevent him from entering Jannah except death.

These are not just for illness — they are daily shields.
Bukhari 5017 · Nasai 9928
SEEKING HELP
When to Seek Ruqyah from Others
If self-ruqyah is not sufficient, seek a trustworthy person who knows the authentic ruqyah. The conditions: Qur'an and authentic duas only — no symbols, no unknown words, no talismans.

The practitioner must have tawakkul in Allah — not in themselves. You may also perform ruqyah on your children, spouse, and family daily.
🛡️
Complete
Ruqyah is your daily armour. The three Quls morning and evening. Ayatul Kursi after every salah. Your protection begins with you.

FOR YOURSELF
For Yourself
Ask for forgiveness of all your sins — known and unknown. Ask for health, strength, tawfiq to worship. Ask for a good end. Ask for firmness on the deen.

Ask for your specific needs right now.
FOR YOUR PARENTS
For Your Parents
رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيرًا
Rabbi irḥamhumā kamā rabbayānī ṣaghīrā
"My Lord, have mercy on them as they raised me when I was small." — Qur'an 17:24

Make this du'a after every salah. If they have passed, this is their ongoing sadaqah jariyah through you.
FOR THIS DUNYA
For This Dunya
Ask for halal provision — rizq, barakah in your wealth and time. Ask for a righteous spouse and children. Ask for a good home. Ask for ease in your work.

Ask for your specific worldly needs by name — Allah loves specificity.
FOR THE AKHIRAH
For the Akhirah
Ask for Jannah al-Firdaws — the highest level. Ask for protection from the punishment of the grave. Ask for protection from the Fire. Ask to see the face of Allah ﷻ. Ask to be with the Prophet ﷺ.

Do not be shy — these are the duas of the Prophets.
FOR THE UMMAH
For the Ummah
Ask for ease for Muslims around the world who are suffering. Ask for unity and guidance for Muslim leaders. Ask for the guidance of those who oppress. Ask for the protection of Muslim children, women, scholars.

"The du'a of a Muslim for his brother in his absence is answered — the angel says: Ameen, and may you have the same."
Sahih Muslim 2733
