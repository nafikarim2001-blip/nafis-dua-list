★ Laylatul Qadr Dua ★
Taught by the Prophet ﷺ to Aisha رضي الله عنها — specifically for Laylatul Qadr
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Allāhumma innaka ʿafuwwun tuḥibbul-ʿafwa faʿfu ʿannī
"O Allah, You are the Pardoner, You love to pardon, so pardon me."
Source: al-Tirmidhi (3513), Ibn Majah (3850) — Sahih
Say in every sajdah · on your commute · before sleep · after every salah
Sayyid al-Istighfar — Master Dua of Forgiveness
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allāhumma Anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā Anta, khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka, wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿtu, aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿtu, abūʾu laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya, wa abūʾu laka bidhanbī faghfir lī, fa innahu lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā Ant
"O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I am upon Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me and I acknowledge my sin. So forgive me, for no one forgives sins except You."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306) — Say morning & evening: dies that day → enters Paradise
Dua of Yunus (AS) — Al-Anbiya 21:87
لَّا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
Lā ilāha illā Anta subḥānaka innī kuntu minaẓ-ẓālimīn
"There is no god but You. Glory be to You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."
Source: Qur'an 21:87
No Muslim supplicates sincerely with this except Allah answers — al-Tirmidhi (Sahih)
For Good in Both Worlds — Al-Baqarah 2:201
رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ
Rabbanā ātinā fid-dunyā ḥasanatan wa fil-ākhirati ḥasanatan wa qinā ʿadhāban-nār
"Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire."

Morning AdhkarAfter Fajr · Seal the morning with remembrance
The morning adhkar are a comprehensive shield for the day. Begin after Fajr salah, ideally before sunrise. They take 10–15 minutes and provide protection, forgiveness, and gratitude all in one.
  • 1
    Ayatul Kursi — 1×
    اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ...
    Recite Ayatul Kursi in full (Al-Baqarah 2:255). A guardian is appointed; Shaytan cannot approach until evening.
    Source: al-Nasāʾi, ʿAmal al-Yawm wal-Laylah (960)
  • 2
    Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas — 3× each
    قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۞ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۞ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ
    Recite each surah 3 times. Protection from all harm for the morning.
    Source: Abu Dawud (5082), al-Tirmidhi (3575)
  • 3
    Sayyid al-Istighfar — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ...
    The Master of Forgiveness. Whoever says this in the morning believing in it, and dies that day, enters Paradise.
    Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (6306)
  • 4
    Protection from All Harm — 3×
    بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
    Bismillāhil-ladhī lā yaḍurru maʿa ismihi shayʾun fil-arḍi wa lā fis-samāʾ wa Huwa as-Samīʿul-ʿAlīm
    "In the name of Allah with whose name nothing on earth or in heaven can cause harm. He is the All-Hearing, All-Knowing."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5088), al-Tirmidhi (3388) — Nothing will harm him that morning
  • 5
    Contentment & Gratitude — 3×
    رَضِيتُ بِاللَّهِ رَبًّا وَبِالْإِسْلَامِ دِينًا وَبِمُحَمَّدٍ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ نَبِيًّا
    Raḍītu billāhi Rabbā wa bil-Islāmi dīnā wa bi-Muḥammadin ṣallallāhu ʿalayhi wa sallama nabiyyā
    "I am pleased with Allah as my Lord, with Islam as my religion, and with Muhammad ﷺ as my Prophet."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5072) — It is incumbent upon Allah to please him
  • 6
    Morning Declaration — 1×
    أَصْبَحْنَا وَأَصْبَحَ الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ...
    Aṣbaḥnā wa aṣbaḥal-mulku lillāh, wal-ḥamdu lillāh, lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah...
    "We have entered the morning and the dominion belongs to Allah... My Lord, I ask You for the good of this day and I seek refuge in You from the evil of this day, from laziness, the misery of old age, the punishment of the Fire, and the punishment of the grave."
    Source: Sahih Muslim (2723)
  • 7
    Protection from the Fire — 7×
    اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي مِنَ النَّارِ
    Allāhumma ajirni minan-nār
    "O Allah, protect me from the Fire." — The Fire will not touch him that day.
    Source: Abu Dawud (5079)
  • 8
    For Complete Wellbeing — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ الْعَفْوَ وَالْعَافِيَةَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ ۙ اللَّهُمَّ اسْتُرْ عَوْرَاتِي وَآمِنْ رَوْعَاتِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ احْفَظْنِي مِنْ بَيْنِ يَدَيَّ وَمِنْ خَلْفِي وَعَنْ يَمِينِي وَعَنْ شِمَالِي وَمِنْ فَوْقِي
    Allāhumma innī asʾalukal-ʿafwa wal-ʿāfiyata fid-dunyā wal-ākhirah · Allāhummastur ʿawrātī wa āmin rawʿātī · Allāhummaḥfaẓnī min bayni yadayya wa min khalfī wa ʿan yamīnī wa ʿan shimālī wa min fawqī
    "O Allah, I ask You for pardon and wellbeing in this world and the next. O Allah, conceal my faults and calm my fears. O Allah, protect me from in front of me, behind me, from my right, my left, and from above me."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5074), Ibn Majah (3871)
  • 9
    Health in Body, Hearing & Sight — 3×
    اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي بَدَنِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي سَمْعِي ۙ اللَّهُمَّ عَافِنِي فِي بَصَرِي ۙ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
    Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī badanī · Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī samʿī · Allāhumma ʿāfinī fī baṣarī · lā ilāha illā Ant
    "O Allah, grant me health in my body. O Allah, grant me health in my hearing. O Allah, grant me health in my sight. There is no god but You."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5090)
  • 10
    Gratitude for Blessings — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ مَا أَصْبَحَ بِي مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ أَوْ بِأَحَدٍ مِنْ خَلْقِكَ فَمِنْكَ وَحْدَكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ ۙ فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ وَلَكَ الشُّكْرُ
    Allāhumma mā aṣbaḥa bī min niʿmatin aw bi-aḥadin min khalqik, fa minka waḥdaka lā sharīka lak, fa lakal-ḥamdu wa lakash-shukr
    "O Allah, whatever blessing I or any of Your creation have reached this morning, is from You alone — no partner. To You belongs all praise and all thanks."
    Source: Abu Dawud (5073) — Whoever says this has given thanks for the day
  • 11
    Tahlīl — 100×
    لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ ۙ لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
    Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah, lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamd, wa Huwa ʿalā kulli shayʾin Qadīr
    "There is no god except Allah alone, no partner. To Him belongs the dominion and all praise, and He has power over all things." — 100 good deeds, 100 sins erased, protection from Shaytan all day.
    Source: al-Bukhari (3293), Muslim (2691)
  • 12
    SubḥānAllāh wa biḥamdih — 100×
    سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ
    Subḥānallāhi wa biḥamdih
    "Glory be to Allah and His praise." — Sins forgiven even if like the foam of the sea.
    Source: al-Bukhari (6405), Muslim (2692)
  • 13
    Allāhumma bika aṣbaḥnā — 1×
    اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا وَبِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا وَبِكَ نَحْيَا وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ النُّشُورُ
    Allāhumma bika aṣbaḥnā wa bika amsaynā wa bika naḥyā wa bika namūtu wa ilaykan-nushūr
    "O Allah, by Your grace we have reached the morning, and by Your grace we reach the evening. By Your grace we live and die, and unto You is the resurrection."
    Source: al-Tirmidhi (3391), Abu Dawud (5068)
  • 14
    Beneficial Knowledge, Provision & Accepted Deeds — after Fajr
    اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا وَرِزْقًا طَيِّبًا وَعَمَلًا مُتَقَبَّلًا
    Allāhumma innī asʾaluka ʿilman nāfiʿan wa rizqan ṭayyiban wa ʿamalan mutaqabbilā
    "O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, good provision, and accepted deeds."
    Source: Ibn Majah (925) — Sahih · Said specifically after Fajr salah
  • 15
    Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm — 1×
    يَا حَيُّ يَا قَيُّومُ بِرَحْمَتِكَ أَسْتَغِيثُ أَصْلِحْ لِي شَأْنِي كُلَّهُ وَلَا تَكِلْنِي إِلَىٰ نَفْسِي طَرْفَةَ عَيْنٍ
    Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm, bi-raḥmatika astaghīth · Aṣliḥ lī shaʾnī kullah, wa lā takilnī ilā nafsī ṭarfata ʿayn
    "O Ever-Living, O Sustainer of All, by Your mercy I seek help. Rectify all my affairs, and do not entrust me to myself even for the blink of an eye."
    Source: al-Nasāʾi in ʿAmal al-Yawm wal-Laylah (46)

Before SleepSunnah sequence · 3 minutes · every night
Step 1 — Ayatul Kursi (Al-Baqarah 2:255)
اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَّهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
Allāhu lā ilāha illā Huwal-Ḥayyul-Qayyūm · lā taʾkhudhuhu sinatun wa lā nawm · lahu mā fis-samāwāti wa mā fil-arḍ · man dhal-ladhī yashfaʿu ʿindahu illā bi-idhnih · yaʿlamu mā bayna aydīhim wa mā khalfahum · wa lā yuḥīṭūna bishayʾin min ʿilmihī illā bimā shāʾ · wasiʿa kursiyyuhus-samāwāti wal-arḍ · wa lā yaʾūduhu ḥifẓuhumā · wa Huwal-ʿAliyyul-ʿAẓīm
"Allah — there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Step 3 — Tahajjud — Opening Dua of Night Prayer
Wake for Tahajjud and recite this before beginning your prayer. The Prophet ﷺ said this every night when rising for the night prayer. It is the most comprehensive dua in the entire Sunnah.
اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ أَنتَ قَيِّمُ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأَرْضِ وَمَن فِيهِنَّ وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ لَكَ مُلْكُ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأَرْضِ وَمَن فِيهِنَّ وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ أَنتَ نُورُ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأَرْضِ وَمَن فِيهِنَّ وَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ أَنتَ الْحَقُّ وَوَعْدُكَ الْحَقُّ وَلِقَاؤُكَ حَقٌّ وَقَوْلُكَ حَقٌّ وَالْجَنَّةُ حَقٌّ وَالنَّارُ حَقٌّ وَالنَّبِيُّونَ حَقٌّ وَمُحَمَّدٌ حَقٌّ وَالسَّاعَةُ حَقٌّ اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ أَسْلَمْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنتُ وَعَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلتُ وَإِلَيْكَ أَنَبتُ وَبِكَ خَاصَمتُ وَإِلَيْكَ حَاكَمتُ فَاغْفِرْ لِي مَا قَدَّمتُ وَمَا أَخَّرتُ وَمَا أَسْرَرتُ وَمَا أَعْلَنتُ أَنتَ الْمُقَدِّمُ وَأَنتَ الْمُؤَخِّرُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلا أَنتَ
Allāhumma lakal-ḥamdu Anta qayyimus-samāwāti wal-arḍi wa man fīhinn. Wa lakal-ḥamdu laka mulkus-samāwāti wal-arḍi wa man fīhinn. Wa lakal-ḥamdu Anta nūrus-samāwāti wal-arḍi wa man fīhinn. Wa lakal-ḥamdu Antal-ḥaqqu wa waʿdukal-ḥaqqu wa liqāʾuka ḥaqqun wa qawluka ḥaqqun wal-jannatu ḥaqqun wan-nāru ḥaqqun wan-nabiyyūna ḥaqqun wa Muḥammadun ḥaqqun was-sāʿatu ḥaqq. Allāhumma laka aslamtu wa bika āmantu wa ʿalayka tawakkaltu wa ilayka anabtu wa bika khāṣamtu wa ilayka ḥākamtu faghfir lī mā qaddamtu wa mā akhkhartu wa mā asrartu wa mā aʿlantu, Antal-muqaddimu wa Antal-muʾakhkhiru lā ilāha illā Ant
"O Allah, to You belongs all praise. You are the Sustainer of the heavens and earth and whoever is in them. To You belongs all praise — Yours is the dominion of the heavens and earth. To You belongs all praise — You are the Light of the heavens and earth. To You belongs all praise — You are the Truth, Your promise is true, meeting You is true, Your word is true, Paradise is true, the Fire is true, the Prophets are true, Muhammad ﷺ is true, and the Hour is true. O Allah, to You I submit, in You I believe, upon You I rely, to You I turn, by You I argue, and to You I bring my case. Forgive me what I have sent ahead and what I have left behind, what I have hidden and what I have declared. You are the Advancer and You are the Delayer — there is no god but You."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (1120), Sahih Muslim (769)
The Prophet ﷺ recited this every night when rising for Tahajjud
Step 4 — Last Two Ayaat of Al-Baqarah (2:285–286)
آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ ۚ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ ۚ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍ مِّن رُّسُلِهِ ۚ وَقَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا ۖ غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ ۝ لَا يُكَلِّفُ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِلَّا وُسْعَهَا ۚ لَهَا مَا كَسَبَتْ وَعَلَيْهَا مَا اكْتَسَبَتْ ۗ رَبَّنَا لَا تُؤَاخِذْنَا إِن نَّسِينَا أَوْ أَخْطَأْنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْنَا إِصْرًا كَمَا حَمَلْتَهُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِنَا ۚ رَبَّنَا وَلَا تُحَمِّلْنَا مَا لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا بِهِ ۖ وَاعْفُ عَنَّا وَاغْفِرْ لَنَا وَارْحَمْنَا ۚ أَنتَ مَوْلَانَا فَانصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ
Āmanar-Rasūlu bimā unzila ilayhi mir-Rabbihī wal-muʾminūn · kullun āmana billāhi wa malāʾikatihī wa kutubihī wa rusulih · lā nufarriqu bayna aḥadim-mir-rusulih · wa qālū samiʿnā wa aṭaʿnā · ghufrānaka Rabbanā wa ilaykal-maṣīr · Lā yukallifullāhu nafsan illā wusʿahā · lahā mā kasabat wa ʿalayhā mak-tasabat · Rabbanā lā tuʾākhidhnā in nasīnā aw akhṭaʾnā · Rabbanā wa lā taḥmil ʿalaynā iṣran kamā ḥamaltahu ʿalal-ladhīna min qablinā · Rabbanā wa lā tuḥammilnā mā lā ṭāqata lanā bih · waʿfu ʿannā waghfir lanā warḥamnā · Anta mawlānā fanṣurnā ʿalal-qawmil-kāfirīn
"The Messenger has believed in what was revealed to him from his Lord, and so have the believers... Our Lord, do not hold us accountable if we forget or err... Pardon us, forgive us, and have mercy upon us. You are our Protector, so help us against the disbelieving people."
Source: Bukhari (5009), Muslim (807) — "They will suffice him." Brought as a gift from beneath Allah's Throne.

Step 7 — Complete Submission Before Sleep
اللَّهُمَّ أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ وَوَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِي إِلَيْكَ وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ لَا مَلْجَأَ وَلَا مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلَّا إِلَيْكَ آمَنْتُ بِكِتَابِكَ الَّذِي أَنزَلْتَ وَبِنَبِيِّكَ الَّذِي أَرْسَلْتَ
Allāhumma aslamtu nafsī ilayk, wa wajjahtu wajhī ilayk, wa fawwaḍtu amrī ilayk, wa alja'tu ẓahrī ilayk, raghbatan wa rahbatan ilayk, lā malja'a wa lā manjā minka illā ilayk, āmantu bikitābikal-ladhī anzalt, wa binabiyyikal-ladhī arsalt
"O Allah, I submit myself to You, turn my face to You, entrust my affairs to You, and place my back against You out of desire and fear of You. There is no refuge and no escape from You except to You. I believe in Your Book which You revealed and Your Prophet whom You sent."
Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (247) — Whoever says this and dies that night dies upon the fiṭrah

Step 9 — Entrusting the Soul to Allah
اللَّهُمَّ خَلَقْتَ نَفْسِي وَأَنتَ تَوَفَّاهَا لَكَ مَمَاتُهَا وَمَحْيَاهَا إِنْ أَحْيَيْتَهَا فَاحْفَظْهَا وَإِنْ أَمَتَّهَا فَاغْفِرْ لَهَا

"O Allah, You created my soul and You take it. To You belongs its death and its life. If You give it life, protect it. And if You cause it to die, forgive it."
Source: Sahih Muslim (2712)

Allāhumma iyyāka naʿbudu · wa laka nuṣallī wa nasjud · wa ilayka nasʿā wa naḥfid · wa narjū raḥmatak · wa nakhshā ʿadhābak · inna ʿadhābaka bil-kuffāri mulḥiq
"O Allah, we seek Your help and ask Your forgiveness, and we believe in You and rely on You. We praise You with all goodness, and we thank You and we are not ungrateful to You. We disown and abandon those who disobey You. O Allah, You alone we worship, and to You we pray and prostrate. To You we hasten and we serve. We hope for Your mercy and we fear Your punishment. Verily, Your punishment shall overtake the disbelievers."
Source: Abu Dawud (1425), al-Bayhaqi — Sahih — Narrated from ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (RA) as the dua the Prophet ﷺ taught for Witr
When to recite: In the final rak'ah of Witr prayer, either before rukuʿ (raising hands with takbir after recitation) or after rukuʿ (before going into sujud) — both positions are established in the Sunnah. Raise your hands while reciting. Witr is one, three, five, or seven rak'ahs — the odd number that concludes your night prayer.

Sadaqat al-Fitr (Zakat al-Fitr) is obligatory (wājib) upon every Muslim who possesses the nisab amount. It must be given before the Eid prayer — giving it after is still valid but the full reward is missed. The Hanafi amount is the equivalent of 2kg of wheat or 4kg of barley, dates or raisins per person in your household. Give it to the poor and needy.

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari (1503), Muslim (984)

Ashura — 10th MuharramFasting and remembrance
The Prophet ﹺ arrived in Madinah and found the Jews fasting on the 10th of Muharram. He asked why and was told: This is the day Allah saved Musa and his people and drowned Pharaoh, so Musa fasted it in gratitude. The Prophet ﹺ said: “We have more right to Musa than you,” and he fasted and commanded fasting. — Sahih al-Bukhari (2004)
The ruling — fast the 9th and 10th

Fasting the 10th of Muharram (Ashura) expiates the minor sins of the past year. The Prophet ﹺ intended to add the 9th of Muharram to distinguish from the Jewish practice. The Hanafi position: it is strongly recommended (mustaḥabb) to fast both the 9th and 10th of Muharram.

Source: Sahih Muslim (1162) for expiation, Muslim (1134) for intention to add the 9th
Intention for the fast of Ashura
نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أَصُومَ غَدًا لِلَّهِ تَعَالَى
Nawaytu an aṣūma ghadan lillāhi taʿālā
“I intend to fast tomorrow for the sake of Allah the Most High.”
Source: Hanafi formulation — the intention is in the heart. Made before Fajr.
⚠️ What has no basis on Ashura

Some people prepare special food, hold gatherings, or give charity specifically on Ashura believing it to be a Sunnah. None of these practices are established in any authentic hadith. The only confirmed act of worship on Ashura is fasting the 9th and 10th of Muharram.

Source: Ibn Taymiyyah, Iqtida al-Sirat al-Mustaqim

Laylat al-Bara‘ah15th Sha‘ban — individual worship
There are narrations mentioning the 15th of Sha‘ban as a night of special mercy and forgiveness. While some scholars have questioned the strength of these narrations, the majority of Hanafi scholars including Ibn Abidin and al-Tahtawi have stated that individual acts of worship — nafl prayer, recitation, istighfar and personal dua — are recommended on this night based on the general principle of seeking good in blessed occasions.
Recommended acts on this night

Spend the night in individual worship: nafl salah, recitation of Qur’an, abundant istighfar, and personal dua. Visit the graves if able — the Prophet ﹺ visited the graveyard of al-Baqi on this night. No congregational gathering, no specific number of rak‘ahs, and no communal celebration has any authentic basis.

Source: Hanafi position — Ibn Abidin, Radd al-Muhtar; al-Tahtawi, Hashiyat Maraqi al-Falah
⚠️ What has no basis on this night

Specific prayers with fixed numbers of rak‘ahs, communal gatherings, special foods, and fireworks on this night have no authentic basis in the Sunnah. Avoid these practices.
